# Design Context — Haze

**Target audience**: Crypto-native testers, developers, and prospective validators evaluating a new privacy L1 during its public testnet phase. Technical enough to know what a Mimblewimble chain is; not designing for retail/mainstream crypto-newcomer audiences.

**Use cases**: Understand what Haze is and why it's different (privacy-by-default, no smart contracts, native NFT/marketplace support) in under a minute; find links to the live wallet, explorer, and marketplace; download the testnet node binary or set up as a validator; check it's still alive/active (not an abandoned project).

**Brand personality/tone**: Refined, atmospheric, quietly confident — not hype-driven, not corporate-enterprise. The existing sibling apps (wallet, explorer, marketplace) already established a "fog/mist" visual language literalizing the "Haze" name: dark-first OKLCH palette (fog/mist/ink neutrals tinted violet-gray, amber as the singular accent, violet as a secondary), Fraunces (italic serif) for display/brand, Public Sans for body, IBM Plex Mono for hashes/technical values. New pages should extend this system, not invent a new one — this homepage's explicit brief is "match our wallet and explorer."

**This page's specific direction**: Glassmorphic treatment (frosted/translucent panels, layered depth) — chosen deliberately to literalize "haze" (fog you can partly see through), not as decorative default. Use sparingly and purposefully: translucency signals depth/layering in the hero and key panels, not applied to every surface.
