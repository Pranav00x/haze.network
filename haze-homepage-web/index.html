Haze is the HTTPS moment for money on public blockchains.

Just as HTTPS made it safe to bank and shop over an open internet, Haze makes it safe to hold and move money on an open chain — amounts and balances stay encrypted and unlinkable, while every block remains publicly verifiable by anyone. Privacy and openness, at the same time, with no trusted intermediary.

Built for the people who'd rather not publish their finances

Confidentiality isn't a feature here. It's the default.

How it stays private

One equation checks the whole block. It never needs to know who.

An output is a Pedersen commitment hiding the amount, a Bulletproof proving the math holds, and a sealed note only the owner can read. There are no balances to update and no addresses to route to.

01Amounts are hiddenCommitments and range proofs verify a spend is valid without ever revealing what it's worth.
02The graph is erasedSpent inputs and outputs cut through — they cancel out before landing on chain.
03History stays smallOnly kernels and the current UTXO set are kept; the chain forgets the rest.
Block validity, in full
inputs − outputs + fees = 0

That's the whole check. If the commitments balance, money is conserved — no need to know who, or how much. Everything else the ledger could have remembered simply isn't stored.

Confidentiality with no architectural compromise.

Haze is its own Layer 1 — one Rust core with no native dependencies, compiling to WebAssembly, Android, iOS, and desktop so every wallet runs identical consensus logic. No trusted setup, no privileged sequencer, no closed chain. Just Ristretto255, Bulletproofs, and full public verifiability.
